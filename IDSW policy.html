Policy Positions

IDSW supports practical, evidence-informed policies that strengthen infectious diseases care, prevention, and public health across Washington State.

Policy Positions Advocacy Actions
These policy positions reflect the Infectious Diseases Society of Washington’s general advocacy priorities. They are intended to guide education, outreach, and engagement on issues affecting patients, clinicians, and communities. Specific advocacy actions may vary over time as new issues emerge.

Access to Infectious Diseases Care

IDSW supports policies that improve timely access to infectious diseases expertise across inpatient, outpatient, urban, rural, and community settings.

  • Support access to specialist consultation for complex infectious diseases cases.
  • Reduce geographic and structural barriers to infectious diseases care.
  • Promote referral pathways that connect patients and clinicians with appropriate expertise.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

IDSW supports policies that promote responsible antimicrobial use and strengthen stewardship programs across hospitals, clinics, and community practice.

  • Encourage investment in stewardship infrastructure, staffing, and collaboration.
  • Promote appropriate antimicrobial use to reduce resistance and improve patient outcomes.
  • Support interdisciplinary stewardship efforts involving physicians, pharmacists, laboratories, and infection prevention teams.

Public Health Infrastructure and Preparedness

IDSW supports strong public health systems and policies that improve surveillance, communication, outbreak response, and preparedness capacity.

  • Support sustainable investment in public health staffing and core infrastructure.
  • Strengthen coordination between healthcare systems, laboratories, and public health agencies.
  • Promote readiness for emerging infectious threats and outbreak response.

Vaccines and Prevention

IDSW supports evidence-based prevention strategies, including vaccination, public education, and community outreach that reduce preventable illness.

  • Promote access to recommended vaccines and preventive services.
  • Support science-based public communication about infectious diseases prevention.
  • Advance prevention strategies that protect individuals and communities across Washington State.

Workforce, Training, and Professional Support

IDSW supports policies that strengthen the infectious diseases workforce, including physicians, pharmacists, microbiology professionals, trainees, and public health partners.

  • Support training pathways, mentorship, and professional development in infectious diseases.
  • Promote recruitment and retention of ID professionals across diverse practice settings.
  • Recognize the value of interdisciplinary expertise in clinical care, prevention, and public health.

Diagnostics and Laboratory Capacity

IDSW supports policies that preserve and strengthen access to high-quality microbiology and infectious diseases diagnostic services.

  • Support timely access to diagnostic testing and laboratory expertise.
  • Recognize the essential role of microbiology in clinical care, stewardship, and public health decision-making.
  • Promote systems that improve diagnostic quality, coordination, and turnaround time.
These positions reflect general advocacy priorities and may be refined over time based on emerging issues, member input, and the needs of patients and communities.
